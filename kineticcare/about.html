The story

Founded in 2019, in the wrong city for desk-jobs.

Bengaluru sits on a compound problem. The average software engineer here spends 8 to 10 hours a day seated. Add a two-hour commute and you have a population whose musculoskeletal system is being renegotiated by its chair.

When Dr. Anjali Rao opened the first KineticCare clinic in Indiranagar in 2019, it was meant to be a five-person practice serving the neighbourhood. Six months in, half her patients were engineers from Koramangala and Whitefield walking in with cervical radiculopathy they'd been told was "stress" — and force-plate tests that proved otherwise.

We expanded what a physiotherapy session looks like. AI motion capture for every first consultation. Force-plate gait analysis for every return-to-sport patient. A dashboard that shows patients their progress in numbers — lumbar flexion in degrees, pain scores on a 0–10 curve, mobility percentages — not in vibes.

Today we've logged 12,427 recovery programs across six specialties. Median recovery time is 3.2× faster than conventional PT. Patient adherence sits at 94%. We think the reason is simple: people stick with programs they can see working.

How we practice

Three principles. Everything else follows.

01

Measurement before narrative.

Every patient gets quantitative baselines on session one — joint kinematics, posture angles, force-plate readings, pain scores. We retest monthly. If the numbers aren't moving, the protocol is wrong, not the patient.

02

Load is medicine.

Rest makes things stiffer, not better. Our protocols progressively load tissue through guided rehabilitation — calibrated to what your body can handle this week, and the week after, and the week after that.

03

Graduate, don't subscribe.

The goal of a physiotherapy program is to end it. We teach patients the why behind every exercise so they can self-manage when they leave. We'd rather lose a subscription than keep you dependent.
